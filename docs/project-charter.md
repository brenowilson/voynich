# Project Charter

## Purpose
Extract reproducible structure from the Voynich Manuscript without presupposing language, cipher, semantics, alphabet or intended function.

## Research stance
The manuscript is treated first as an observed physical and graphical object. A representation is acceptable when its construction is explicit, reversible where possible, uncertainty-aware and testable under perturbation.

The project does not prohibit linguistic, cryptographic or semantic hypotheses. It prevents them from defining the canonical observational layer.

## Meaning of independence
Independence does not mean ignoring prior scholarship. It means that prior scholarship cannot determine our segmentation, glyph inventory, spacing rules or canonical units before our own observations are frozen.

## Structure before meaning
We seek regularities, invariants, transitions, symmetries, recurrences, scales and operator-dependent modes. A discovered structure is not assigned meaning merely because it resembles a known object.

## Examples and analogies
Examples are communication devices. They approximate an intended abstraction and are not literal commitments.

An example mentioning letters, words, eigenvalues, eigenvectors, constants, functions or semantic units does not assert that those objects exist in the manuscript. It indicates that some unknown structure may admit a useful representation or comparison.

Before formalization, ask what structural intention the example is pointing toward. A literal interpretation becomes valid only through an explicit definition or decision record.

## Success criteria
- independent and auditable corpus construction;
- reproducible computations from immutable inputs;
- explicit dependence on segmentation and representation choices;
- out-of-sample validation;
- comparison against strong null models;
- publication of negative as well as positive results;
- clean isolation of external comparisons.
