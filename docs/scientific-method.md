# Scientific Method

## 1. Observation
Record source identity, coordinates, geometry, connected components, candidate groupings and uncertainty. Avoid semantic labels.

## 2. Representation
Every representation must specify its units, transformations, information loss and invariances. No representation is canonical merely because it is conventional.

## 3. Freeze
Freeze a declared corpus subset before examining external transliterations or optimizing a hypothesis against it.

## 4. Operator construction
An operator must be derived from an explicit rule on frozen data. Its domain, codomain, normalization, boundary conditions and symmetry properties must be documented.

## 5. Analysis
Report full spectra or justified summaries, numerical tolerances, degeneracies, stability under perturbation and sensitivity to preprocessing.

## 6. Null models
Use nulls preserving different levels of structure: marginal frequencies, line lengths, degree sequences, spatial density, transition counts and local geometry.

## 7. Validation
Separate exploratory and confirmatory work. Hold out pages or regions. Test transfer across folios, sections, hands and acquisition conditions without assuming those categories are fundamental.

## 8. External comparison
External transliterations and published claims are evaluated only against named frozen releases. Disagreement is data, not a correction command.

## 9. Reproducibility
Each result records source hashes, corpus freeze, schema versions, code commit, parameters, random seeds and environment.
